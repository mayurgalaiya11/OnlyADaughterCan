WHY AND HOW DOES ADULT PNEUMOCOCCAL DISEASE
POSE A RISK TO YOUR PARENTS?

Pneumococcal bacteria spreads through coughing, sneezing or direct contact.

When an individual turns 50, they are at a higher risk of Pneumococcal disease; which makes your parents more susceptible to it.

Also certain medical conditions, such as diabetes, chronic lung disease or chronic heart disease could put your parents at an increased risk.

How can I protect my parents? 

Adult Pneumococcal disease can be prevented by immunization. According to the Centre for Disease Control, USA (CDC), the best way to protect your parents is to get them vaccinated.

You can also consult your treating physician, chest specialist or diabetologist.

Adult Pneumococcal Immunization is recommended by global scientific bodies such as the Advisory Committee of Immunization and Practice (ACIP) and American Diabetes Association (ADA).

As per the World Health Organization (WHO) guidelines, though vaccines such as haemophilus influenzae type B vaccine or Pneumococcal vaccine cannot prevent new Coronavirus, they should be recommended to protect against respective respiratory illnesses.
